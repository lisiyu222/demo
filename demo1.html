A Scatter Map of Public vs. Private Universities in US

This is a description of the scatter map of public and private universities. On this map, you will see two different colored dots representing different types of universities. The blue dots represent public schools, which are funded and operated by the government. This means they might have more sources of funding, typically charge lower fees, and often cater to a large number of students.

On the other hand, the red dots represent private schools. Private schools are supported by private funds, donations, or tuition fees. These schools may offer a more specialized education, closely associated with a particular religion or educational philosophy. Since they are not government-funded, private schools might have higher tuition fees, but they may also offer more scholarships or financial aid opportunities.

By looking at this scatter map, you can easily distinguish between public and private universities. This helps to understand the distribution of higher education in a region or country, as well as the ratio between public and private institutions.
